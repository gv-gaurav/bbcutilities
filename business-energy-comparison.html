What Information Do You Need for a Commercial Energy Comparison?

When you're checking out different gas and electricity tariffs there are two important things to think about:

  • The cost per KWH - This is how much you pay for every bit of electricity and gas you use. Different suppliers charge different amounts for this.
  • The Standing Charge - This is a small fee that you pay every day to have electricity and gas supplied to your home. It can vary from one supplier to another, and it's important to consider.
  • We will take care of your renewal or switch.

Sometimes, you might see a plan that offers a cheap 'cost per kWH,' but it might have a higher 'daily standing charge.' That can make your overall energy bill more expensive. So, it's important to look at both of these costs to figure out if a plan is a good deal.

Domestic Energy Vs Commercial Energy

One of the main differences between home and commercial energy is the rules that apply to each group of customers. Home energy customers have the flexibility to switch their energy plans at any time, but there might be a fee if they do it before the contract ends. On the other hand, if you're a commercial energy customer, you can only change to a new supplier during your renewal period, which usually starts between one to six months before your current contract ends.

Another significant difference is how you choose your energy plan. Home customers can easily select from standard, ready-made energy plans, but for businesses, it's a bit more complex. To find the right energy deal for your company, you have to approach each supplier individually and request a customized quote. This allows businesses to get deals that match their unique requirements. However, it means you need to contact each supplier separately to compare rates and ensure you're getting a competitive business energy deal.
